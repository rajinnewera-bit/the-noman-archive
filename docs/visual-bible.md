## Noman

### Noman Character Bible v1.0

This section defines the fixed visual and behavioral language for Noman across all archive pages, future chapter visualizations, cinematic references, dossier material, and recovered evidence treatments. It is a production document, not interpretive prose. The goal is consistency, restraint, and psychological accuracy.

#### 1. Noman – Core Identity

- Full name reference: Noman.
- Date of birth: 25 August 1987.
- Age reference: 38 as of June 2026, turning 39 on 25 August 2026.
- He is not framed as a conventional hero, antihero, or thriller archetype. He should read visually as a man who has spent too long observing life from slightly behind it.
- His defining trait is not aggression, glamour, or mystery-for-style. It is perception.
- He is an observer formed by isolation, social hesitation, and interior overdevelopment.
- He does not search for answers in a triumphant sense. He searches for better questions.
- His emotional truth is buried behind control. Most people read him incorrectly as cold, detached, or heartless.
- The real Noman exists behind a defensive wall built from silence, memory, suspicion, and pattern recognition.
- His central fear is not simple death. It is dying before understanding what people, desire, betrayal, memory, and existence actually are.

#### 2. Physical Appearance

- Height: 5 ft 6 in.
- Weight: approximately 73 kg.
- Build: lean with slight muscular definition; functional rather than athletic.
- Frame: compact, grounded, credible, human.
- Skin tone: brown, not too dark.
- Eyes: black with a slight brown warmth visible only under close or soft light.
- His eyes should often look older than the rest of his face.
- Hair: short, natural, never styled in a conspicuous or fashionable way.
- Beard: subtle stubble, maintained through neglect rather than grooming precision.
- Dominant hand: right.
- Distinguishing mark: wing tattoo on the right arm.
- He should never appear polished, gym-sculpted, or cosmetically dramatic.
- He must feel lived-in, sleep-deprived at times, and mentally overactive.

#### 3. Clothing and Accessories

- Home clothing palette: black, brown, or grey.
- Home default: plain round-neck T-shirt, shorts, barefoot.
- Home styling rule: comfort first, no decorative layering, no effort to impress.
- Outside clothing: plain collared T-shirts or plain round-neck T-shirts.
- Bottoms outside: denim jeans, black jeans, or cargo pants.
- Footwear outside: Crocs or white sports shoes.
- Accessories are fixed and should not drift between interpretations:
- Silver ring with red coral stone on the ring finger of the right hand.
- Golden neck chain.
- No watch.
- Clothing should feel repeat-worn, believable, and practical.
- Avoid logos, graphic statements, luxury styling, trend-driven fashion, or anything too curated.
- His wardrobe should communicate a man who values utility, privacy, and psychological space over presentation.

#### 4. Behaviour and Habits

- He is quiet, but not passive.
- He often delays speech by observation.
- His stutter is important to his development and should inform how he occupies silence.
- He learned early that speaking could expose weakness, so he became skilled at watching.
- As a child he was skinny and was picked on at school; this helped form the observer identity.
- He notices before he responds.
- He often holds still longer than other people are comfortable with.
- He can appear emotionally unreadable in social space.
- He likes tea.
- He mostly drinks beer.
- Weed belongs to his private thinking ritual and should be treated as interior behavior, not counterculture styling.
- He loves silence, but music matters to him when it contains weight, truth, and lyrical confrontation.
- Core music references:
- “The Day That Never Comes” by Metallica.
- “The Sound of Truth” by As I Lay Dying.
- He sometimes stands before a mirror, looks into his own eyes, and asks: “Who are you?”
- This act should be treated as ritual self-confrontation, not vanity.

#### 5. Smoking Rules

- Cigarette type is fixed: Gold Flake Indie Mint.
- Size and appearance: medium-sized white cigarette.
- Smoking is never to be framed as glamour, coolness, seduction, or rebellion branding.
- It appears primarily in three states:
- During intense thinking.
- After lunch.
- At night on the balcony.
- Smoking is tied to mental processing, not performance.
- The cigarette is often forgotten in his fingers while his mind runs elsewhere.
- Ash may grow long because attention leaves the body before it leaves the thought.
- Smoking scenes should emphasize pause, habit, interior pressure, and suspended time.
- If shown visually, the cigarette must feel ordinary, repetitive, and slightly self-destructive.

#### 6. Inner Voice

- Noman’s inner voice is his own voice, not a supernatural entity and not a separate character design language.
- It appears when it wants, not when he needs it.
- It is not comforting.
- It usually emerges at night, in isolation, or in moments of cognitive overreach.
- It reveals the practical, ugly, unsentimental side of people and situations.
- It cuts through self-deception.
- It should sound precise, direct, psychologically invasive, and difficult to ignore.
- Visually, the inner voice should never be represented through obvious fantasy language, horror clichés, or stylized possession imagery.
- The correct tone is intimate menace through self-recognition.
- The audience should feel that Noman is being confronted by a version of himself that has removed illusion.

#### 7. Emotional Architecture

- Outer layer: restraint.
- Second layer: fatigue.
- Third layer: suspicion.
- Fourth layer: hunger for meaning.
- Core layer: a deeply guarded human vulnerability that very few people ever see.
- He is not empty. He is overcontained.
- He is not emotionless. He is structurally defended.
- He is not cynical for style. His doubt comes from repeated observation of contradiction.
- He is drawn toward understanding even when understanding may damage him.
- He fears the unknown, but he also cannot stop moving toward it.
- He is emotionally shaped by contradiction:
- He wants truth, but truth often isolates him.
- He wants closeness, but distrust interrupts intimacy.
- He wants meaning, but meaning keeps retreating into complexity.
- He wants to understand people, but understanding them often reveals ugliness.

#### 8. Things Noman Notices

- Eyes before words.
- Hands before explanations.
- Micro-movements before declarations.
- Silence before speech.
- Delay before reaction.
- Inconsistency between face and intention.
- Lies in rhythm, not just in content.
- The soul behind the body, not just the body itself.
- He reads motive through fracture.
- He is attentive to what people avoid, what they repeat, what they over-explain, and what they cannot hold still.
- He notices emotional weather in rooms.
- He notices the difference between performance and presence.
- His perception should feel forensic, intuitive, and earned.

#### 9. Visual Continuity Rules

- Noman must always remain human-scale.
- Never beautify him into a stylized noir icon or fashionable dark protagonist.
- His stillness is as important as his movement.
- Eye focus matters more than pose.
- His presence should carry accumulated thought.
- The ring, neck chain, right-arm tattoo, stubble, and no-watch rule must remain consistent.
- Home scenes should favor barefoot grounding, quiet fabrics, and minimal visual clutter in wardrobe.
- Outside scenes should preserve plain, unfussy clothing with no sudden style inflation.
- Cigarette handling must remain consistent with right-hand dominance unless a specific blocking reason exists.
- Mirror scenes, balcony scenes, and silent observation scenes should feel like recurring identity checkpoints.
- If Noman is framed in conversation, his listening should often be more visually important than the other person’s speaking.

#### 10. Things Never To Do

- Never make him look like a gangster, model, influencer, or fashion-led antihero.
- Never romanticize smoking.
- Never make weed use feel comedic, performative, or culturally decorative.
- Never portray him as emotionless simply because he is quiet.
- Never over-style his apartment behavior into “cool loneliness.”
- Never give him flashy jewelry beyond the fixed ring and chain.
- Never add a watch.
- Never change or omit the right-arm wing tattoo without an explicit story reason.
- Never turn the inner voice into a horror gimmick, voiceover cliché, or supernatural trope.
- Never frame him as someone chasing answers with certainty.
- Never reduce him to depression aesthetics.
- Never make him speak more than his psychology supports.
- Never confuse silence with emptiness.
- Never forget that his defining motion is observation.
